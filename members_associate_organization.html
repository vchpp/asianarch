Associate Organization Members:


 
Korean Community Center of the East Bay (KCCEB)

KCCEB was established in 1977 to serve the needs of the first large wave of Korean immigrants in the 1970’s who were transitioning into American life. KCCEB is the only organization in the Bay Area to provide culturally and linguistically appropriate programming for the most vulnerable segments of the Korean American community(Click here for website www.kcceb.org.)

 
Asian and Pacific Islander American Health Forum (APIAHF)

Our mission is to influence policy, mobilize communities, and strengthen programs and organizations to improve the health of Asian Americans, Native Hawaiians, and Pacific Islanders.(Click here for website.)

Asian Pacific Partners for Empowerment Advocacy and Leadership (APPEAL)

Our mission is to champion social justice and achieve parity and empowerment for Asian Americans, Native Hawaiians and other Pacific Islanders by supporting and mobilizing community-led movements through advocacy and leadership development on critical public health issues. (Click here for website.)

 

 

Association of Asian Pacific Community Health Organizations (AAPCHO)

Our mission is to serve Asian Americans, Native Hawaiians, and other Pacific Islanders. We are dedicated to promoting advocacy, collaboration, and leadership that improves the health status and access of these medically underserved communities. (Click here for website.)

 

UC AAPI
Policy MRP
UC Asian American and Pacific Islander Policy Multi-campus Research Program

Our mission is to foster policy-relevant research on AAPI issues important to California. By funding and coordinating applied social science and policy research on topics relevant to California’s growing AAPI populations, the MRP serves as a bridge linking UC researchers to community organizations, the media, and California elected officials and their staff. These activities demonstrate the University of California’s commitment to integrate research, teaching, and community outreach in ways that inform and enlighten public discourse on important public policy issues.
