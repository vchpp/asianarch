Core Organization Members:


Asian Health Services (AHS)

Our mission is to serve and advocate for the medically underserved, including the immigrant and refugee Asian community, and to assure equal access to health care services regardless of income, insurance status, language, or culture. (Click here for website.)

 

Chinatown Public Health Center (CPHC)

Our mission is to provide quality and cost-effective primary care to assigned patients and to promote the health of San Franciscans of all ages, with expertise and competence serving immigrants. (Click here for website.)

Chinese Community Health Care Associate (CCHCA)

Chinese Community Health Care Association (CCHCA) understands you and your needs. Established in 1982 as a non-profit Medical Group, CCHCA has been providing the people of San Francisco with quality service and culturally competent health care through our network of highly skilled physicians. We have conveniently located physicians available in a full range of medical and surgical specialties. Services are offered in a variety of Asian languages, such as Cantonese, Mandarin, etc. In addition to providing accessible health care to the citizens of San Francisco, CCHCA is also involved in the community. In partnership with the Asian American Medical Group (AAMG), CCHCA provides its patients with access to in-person health education and online resources found at Asian American Medical Group (AAMG) Health Education Resources, promoting healthy living in the Asian community and beyond. CCHCA also awards grants that help fund programs geared towards educating the community on health related topics. Grant receipients are selected by a board of physicians and community advisors who evaluate the programs' quality and objectives. Applications are open to the public and community organizations alike. Application information and dates will be posted on our website and published in local newspapers.
